Enigma Cryptanalysis

One of the weaknesses of the Engima machine was that cryptanalysts could ignore the plugboard settings while trying to work out the wheel settings. Once they had those, they could turn their attention to the plugs. In this activity, we will assume we have figured out the wheel settings, and then make guesses to figure out the plugboad settings.

Make use of one of the following Enigma simulators:

  • 101 Computing (This one is very pretty.) To access the settings, click on one of the rotor wheels. Be sure to click Apply Settings when you are done.
  • Daniel Palloks (Doesn't have the look of the actual machine, but a very clean website, and allows you to paste in the messages.)

In all of the examples use the initial set up below. (The messages do not use Message Settings.)

  • Enigma: M3
  • Reflector (Umkehrwalze): B
  • Wheel order (Walzenlage): III, I, II
  • Ring Settings (Ringstellung): A A A (1, 1, 1)
  • Indicator Settings (Grundstellung): A C C

With the rotor settings as above, and the plugboard settings unknown, our ciphertext is:

KVMDP JMNZH IWLRO QJPRD DAHJB GR

We now type this into the Engima with the correct rotor settings and no plugs. We get:

XHAAN IGMEI SCOMP LAXAL YKACU RA

We now look for pieces of words. To help spot these, remove the spaces:

XHAANIGMEISCOMPLAXALYKACURA

We might guess the word "ENIGMA" appears:

XHAANIGME ISCOMPLAXALYKACURA
ENIGMA

We guess that one of the plugs is AE. We set this plug and type the ciphertext in again:

XHEEN IGMAI SCOMP LEXEL YSECU RE

Making some guesses about the word breaks:

XHE ENIGMA IS COMPLEXELY SECURE

The first word is probabaly "the", which could result with a TX plug. This would also make sense for the occurence of X later. We get:

THE ENIGMA IS COMPLETELY SECURE

It probably goes without saying that some trial and error is needed in general. Sometimes guesses that look good intially turn out to cause problems later, and we have to backtrack.
